A Journey Through Riddles

Once upon a time in a quaint village, there lived a wise old owl who loved to challenge travelers with riddles. One day, a curious wanderer decided to seek the owl's wisdom.

Riddle 1: What has keys but can't open locks?

A piano.

Impressed, the wanderer continued on, and soon came across another challenge.

Riddle 2: What can travel around the world while staying in a corner?

A stamp.

Feeling clever, the wanderer pressed onward, eager to hear more riddles.

Riddle 3: I speak without a mouth and hear without ears. I have no body, but I come alive with wind. What am I?

An echo.

Each riddle brought new excitement, and soon the wanderer faced another challenge.

Riddle 4: The more of this there is, the less you see. What is it?

Darkness.

With a grin, the wanderer knew they were getting closer to the end of their adventure.

Riddle 5: What is so fragile that saying its name breaks it?

Silence.

With the final riddle solved, the wanderer thanked the wise old owl and continued their journey, enriched with knowledge and joy from the riddles encountered along the way.
